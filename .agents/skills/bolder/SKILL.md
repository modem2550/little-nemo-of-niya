Increase visual impact and personality in designs that are too safe, generic, or visually underwhelming.

## Key Principles

**Assess the design**: Identify what makes it feel boring—generic fonts, low contrast, predictable layouts, no motion, flat hierarchy.

**Plan amplification**: Choose a personality direction (elegant drama, playful energy, dark moody) and increase contrast across typography, color, spacing, and composition.

**Amplify systematically**:
- **Typography**: Replace generic fonts with distinctive choices; use extreme scale jumps (3-5x)
- **Color**: Increase saturation, introduce bold unexpected combinations (avoid purple-blue AI clichés)
- **Spacing**: Use dramatic white space (100-200px gaps), break the grid, create asymmetry
- **Visual Effects**: Dramatic shadows, texture layers, custom shapes (NOT glassmorphism)
- **Motion**: Staggered animations, scroll effects, satisfying micro-interactions
- **Composition**: Clear focal points, diagonal flows, unusual proportions (70/30, 80/20)

**Critical rules**:
- Bold ≠ chaotic. It means distinctive and confident
- Never sacrifice readability or accessibility
- Don't make everything bold (then nothing stands out)
- Avoid AI slop: cyan gradients, neon accents, generic effects
- Keep it functional—impact without purpose is just decoration

**The test**: Would someone immediately recognize this as AI-made? If yes, start over. Bold means memorable and distinctive, not derivative.
